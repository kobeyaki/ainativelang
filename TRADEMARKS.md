# Trademark Policy

This document describes the trademark and branding policy for AI Native Lang (AINL).

## Reserved Marks

Unless otherwise stated, the following names and branding elements are reserved by the project owner:

- `AINL`
- `AI Native Lang`
- official logos, badges, icons, and related branding assets

The open-source and content licenses used in this repository do **not** grant trademark rights.

## Permitted Uses

You may generally make truthful, descriptive references to the project, including:

- referring to the project by name
- stating factual compatibility with AINL
- discussing, reviewing, benchmarking, or comparing the project
- linking to the official project
- accurately describing a fork as being based on or derived from AINL

These references must be accurate and must not be misleading.

## Prohibited Uses Without Permission

Without prior written permission, you may not:

- present a fork, product, service, model pack, or distribution as the official AINL project
- imply sponsorship, endorsement, certification, partnership, or affiliation by the project owner
- use official logos, badges, or branding assets for your own project, service, or distribution
- use confusingly similar names, marks, domains, package names, or branding for commercial or public offerings
- market a product or service in a way that is likely to confuse users about whether it is official

## Forks and Modified Versions

Forks are allowed under the applicable licenses for the open portions of the project. However, forks and modified versions should take reasonable steps to avoid user confusion.

In particular:

- do not describe a fork as the official AINL project
- do not use official logos or branding unless you have permission
- use naming and presentation that make the fork's independent status clear
- if the fork diverges materially, consider renaming it to reduce confusion

Truthful statements such as "Forked from AINL," "Based on AINL," or "Compatible with AINL" are generally acceptable if they are accurate and not misleading.

## Domains, Package Names, and Public Branding

Do not register or use domains, package names, repository names, organization names, account names, or branding identifiers that are likely to cause confusion about whether your project, service, or distribution is official, endorsed, or maintained by the AINL project owner.

## Fair Descriptive Use

Nothing in this policy is intended to prohibit fair, nominative, or otherwise lawful descriptive reference to the project.

## Contact

For trademark, branding, naming, logo, certification, or official-use questions:

- Open a GitHub issue/discussion requesting trademark contact.
- Avoid posting confidential trademark/licensing terms in public threads.

## Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated over time. Updated versions should be published through the official project repository or official project site and will apply prospectively.
