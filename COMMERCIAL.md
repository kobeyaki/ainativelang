# Commercial Use and Enterprise Offerings

AI Native Lang (AINL) uses an **open-core** model.

Designated open-core components are made available under the licenses declared in this repository, including `LICENSE` and, where applicable, `LICENSE.docs`. Those licenses govern the use of the open-core components.

In addition to the open-core project, separate commercial offerings may be made available under independent commercial terms.

## Open-Core Scope

Unless otherwise noted, the open core may include items such as:

- language specifications and grammar references
- parser, compiler, interpreter, or runtime core components
- CLI and baseline developer tooling
- examples, tutorials, and baseline conformance or evaluation utilities
- reference implementations and other materials explicitly designated as open-core

The exact scope of the open core is determined by the license notices and documentation accompanying each repository, package, module, artifact, or distribution.

## Examples of Commercial Offerings

Separate commercial offerings may include, for example:

- hosted AINL platform services
- managed runtime or deployment services
- enterprise orchestration and governance modules
- compliance, audit, and advanced policy tooling
- premium observability, debugging, or evaluation dashboards
- enterprise connectors and proprietary integrations
- premium model packs, checkpoints, or evaluation suites
- SLA-backed support, onboarding, consulting, training, or custom engineering

## Separate Terms Apply

Any proprietary modules, hosted services, premium assets, enterprise features, support plans, or other non-open-core offerings are governed by separate commercial terms and are **not** included under the open-core licenses unless explicitly stated otherwise in writing.

Use of the open-core components does **not** grant any right to access, receive, copy, redistribute, host, sublicense, or use any proprietary offering made available under separate commercial terms.

## No Implied Right to Commercial Offerings

Use of the open-core code does not by itself grant any right to:

- proprietary enterprise modules
- premium model or evaluation assets
- hosted or managed services
- commercial support or SLA programs
- internal tools not released under an open license
- official certification, endorsement, or partner status

## Trademark and Branding

Use of open-core code does **not** grant trademark or branding rights.

See [TRADEMARKS.md](./TRADEMARKS.md) for rules governing the use of the AINL name, branding, logos, and related marks.
